Holly River State Park


Webster County, West Virginia
    The Park covers 8101 acres (3278 hectares) in a narrow valley surrounded by 2800 ft. (853 meter) mountains. In absence of information of total average yearly precipitation, note that of nearby Kumbrabow State Forest is 64 inches (163 cm) as repored by the State. Attention is also drawn to Strausbaugh and Core (1977), who list an average yearly value of 63 inches (160 cm.) for nearby Pickens. Bedrock is dominated by the Kanawha and Allegheny Formations, both of Pennsylvanian age. That of the former is dominated by sandstone, but also contains siltstone, shale and coal. The latter is a cyclic sequence, consisting of sandstone, siltstone, shale and limestone (Cardwell et al, 1968). The latter is of particular significance with respect to the richness and diversity of the flora and fauna, since limestone soils greatly favor these.
