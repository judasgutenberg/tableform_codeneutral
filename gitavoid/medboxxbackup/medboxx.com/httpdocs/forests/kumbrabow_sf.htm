Kumbrabow State Forest

Kumbrabow State Forest (38.64235N, 80.077801W) is a 9,474 acre (3834 hectare) tract located in Randolph County, West Virginia, at the western edge of the Allegheny Highlands, atop Rich Mountain, at elevations ranging between 2300 and 3930 ft. (701- 1198 meters) asl. However, most of the Forest lies above 3000 ft. (914 meters). Rocks in the area are dominated by the Kanawha and New River Formations, both of Pennsylvanian age. These formations are dominated by sandstones, but also contain siltstone, shale and coal (Cardwell et al, 1968). Average annual precipitation is 64 inches (163 cm.), according to the Forest Management Plan. Attention is also drawn to Strausbaugh and Core (1977) who list an average yearly precipitation value of 63 inches (160 cm.) for nearby Pickens. The high precipitation and average elevation result in a rich forest characterized by many northern and montane species, and, according to the State, forms a "showcase for Black Cherry and Red Spruce."

Summary Comments

    Some northern / montane species from the inventories are Red Spruce, Trembling Aspen, Yellow Birch, Smooth Serviceberry, Witch Hobble, Fire Cherry, Choke Cherry, Canada Mayflower, White Wood Sorrel, Dwarf Ginseng and Mountain Aster. On the other hand, soil richness is indicated by such species as Black Walnut, Butternut, Redbud and Big Bluestem. Both characteristics are greatly favored by the abundant moisture which brings about the cool environment ordinarily associated with higher elevations or deep valleys adjacent to such elevations. However, richness and diversity, conspicuous as they appear to be, are inferior to those at nearby Holly River State Park, where limestone soils are more common.
