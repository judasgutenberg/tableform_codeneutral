Tomlinson Run State Park


Hancock County, West Virginia

Introduction

    The Park consists of 1398 acres (566 ha.) along Tomlinson Run, at an elevation of 1200 ft. (366 meters) with overhanging cliffs of sandstone and shale. Bedrock consists of Allegheny Formation and Conemaugh Group, both of Pennsylvanian age. The former is a cyclic sequence of sandstone, siltstone, shale, limestone and coal., while the latter consists of red and gray shale, siltstone and sandstone, with thin limestone and coal beds (Cardwell et al, 1968). Mean annual July temperatures lie between 72 and 74 deg. F (22 and 23 C.), while normal annual precipitation lies between 37 and 42 inches (94 and 107 Cm.). The regional forest is classified as Appalachian Oak (data taken from summaries by Buckelew and Hall, 1994). The reader is also referred to Strausbaugh and Core (1977). These temperatures lie above and the precipitation considerably below those in the region of Kumbrabow State Forest and Holly River State Park, for example. This is also in agreement with the lower elevations at Tomlinson Run compared to those of Kumbrabow and Holly River. In addition, the latter areas apparently contain a greater variety of habitats.

Comments

    The area is low in northern / montane species including canopy trees such as Red Spruce, Yellow Birch,and Trembling Aspen, which are apparently missing. Among understory trees and shrubs the inventory is mixed. Pussy Willow, Red Elderberry, and other northerners occur. However a number of these, such as Pussy Willow, are confined to-usually calcareous- wetlands. The presence of abundant limestone here contributes greatly to the impressive richness and diversity of the flora.
