Homestead 3

 

May 24  Planted about half of my Traverse soy beans ( setting 32 for soy beans ) in the hill field using no fertilizer  or inoculant. I put the hoses down to the slope garden for the year-early I guess, but I needed them since the flea beetles are bad on the cabbage and fast growth and wet conditions suppress them. I also watered in the creek garden, where all the beans are coming up now.

May 25  I watered cabbage, spinach etc. in the slope garden with the hose early this morning. Planted the rest of the Traverse soy bean seeds in the hill field using no fertilizer or inoculant. Planted 2 hills of Small Sugar pumpkin in the creek garden over chicken manure (partly fresh ). 20 eggs today.

May 26  I did a lot of weeding and watering in both gardens. I transplanted  7 Buirpee's  VF tomato plants in the slope garden. 25 eggs today. We gave away 5 doz. to the nursery.

May 27  Sold 6 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. Bought 10 lbs of Agripro soy bean seeds from Wetzels in Waynesboro. We had some showers in the afternoon. 23 eggs today.

May 28  Heavy rain all last night and rain most of the day, with very heavy showers about 4 PM. I weeded and thinned in both gardens. 27 eggs today.

May 29  Sold 3 doz. eggs at 80c a doz, I cut some lawn hay today for the first time this year. Wright's stallion got into our pasture and bred Candy who was in heat., broke a number of fences, got into neighbor's gardens etc. 24 eggs today.

May 30 I cut the rest of the front lawn hay and hauled it all in, 4 WB in all. I also hoed in the garden. We had a short shower this evening.

May 31 Planted 11 Early Prolific sweet pepper plants in the creek garden using no fertilizer. I also planted 4 more Burpee's VF tomatoes in the same garden. I froze 2 qts of spinach. Wild and tame strawberries are ripening. We have gone back to bathing and injecting Jupiter because of his castration wound. 32 eggs today.

June 1 We have been having quite a bit of light rain off and on yesterday, last night and today. I did a quite a bit of weeding in the slope garden. 19 eggs today.

June 2  it rained most of the night and I did a quite a bit of weeding. 28 eggs today. Bought 100 each of corn, oats and mash.

June 3  Sold 4 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. Planted 2 short rows of Roma VF tomato plants ( no stakes ) using no fertilizer. I also did a lot of hoeing in both gardens. We had a heavy shower this evening.

June 4  Our 13 J. J. Warren chicks arrived today from Harrisonburg. I placed them in the brooder house with a light, although it is warm enough without it. More rain this evening. 20 eggs today.

June 5  I picked potato beetle larvae, which are quite bad in the slope garden. Don and I weeded and hoed corn in the hill field. Flea beetles are very bad in the slope garden ( cabbage ), but not in the creek garden. I planted the rest of the band of the Red Cored Chanteny carrots in the slope garden using no fertilizer. Rabbits [? ] are eating a lot of my soy beans in the hill field.

June 6  Today our new goat Brenda, who cost $175, was delivered. Betty gave $10 for delivery in addition. Gus, Don and I hoed corn, although it rained lightly most of the day. Gus and Betty took Honey to the vet who diagnosed "congestion" ( lungs ) and gave 2 shots. ~20 eggs today.

June 7  I cut more lawn hay on the north and south sides of the house. Don, Gus and I  hoed corn. I froze 2 qts. of spinach. We are giving Honey 4 cc/day of Tylan [?] for her congestion. 20 eggs today.

June 8   Sold 4 doz. eggs at 80 c a doz. Hauled in 2 WB of hay. The boys finished hoeing the corn.

June 9  I hoed lots in the slope garden-potatoes, beans, rhubarb, horseradish etc. as well as check for bean beetles, of which I killed 3. i also hoed beans in the creek garden. 23 eggs today.

June 10  Heavy shower last night and morning. i weeded some in the gardens. ~18 eggs today. It is very hot and humid. Gave Honey 2 transmisal [?] wormer tablets.

June 11   I cut hay across the stream on the flood plain. I also did a quite a bit of hoeing. 21 eggs today. The weather was pleasantly warm today. I also picked potato beetles, noting lots of adults,

June 12  I pulled weeds in the soy bean rows and we hauled in 3 WB loads of hay from the flood plain. We had a very light short rain this morning, but this afternoon is dry and warm.

June 13 I hoed soy beans and potatoes in the hill field. I also hoed the creek garden. ~24 eggs today and it is very warm. Don is starting a garden on PP. We stopped shooting Honey yesterday.

June 14 I hoed extensively soy beans and potatoes in the hill field and checked for potato beetles and eggs, finding few. I also hoed in the slope garden and checked for potato and bean beetles, finding a quite a few of the former and less of the latter. No bean beetle larvae or eggs have yet been observed. 24 eggs today. It was hot and humid.

June 15 It was very warm already in the morning. I hoed soy beans and thinned corn, which I gave to Leo each day as part of his greens. The corn in the hill field is already over my knees. The boys began their second round of corn hoeing and I patched the barn in the cool of the evening. I also hoed in the slope garden and picked peas. I'm trying to identify mushrooms, which are out after recent rains. 24 eggs today.

June 16 Hoagie took 6 doz. eggs to the nursery.  I hoed soy beans, weeded cucumbers. I spray Dipel on the cabbage. Bought 100 lbs each of corn, oats and mash.

June 17 I hoed soy beans, picked and froze 3 pts. of peas. I'm feeding the goats lots of corn thinnings now. I watered cabbage in the slope garden this evening. We also hauled down a load of mixed clover ( bush, sweet and red ) from the hill field.

June 18 Hoagie took 6 doz. eggs to another nursery-since we can't sell them. Hoed soy beans as usual. Don, with Gus' help planted carrots in his 7' x 12' PP garden.

June 19 Hoed soy beans, watered corn in slope garden and mulched tomatoes in the same garden. 17 eggs today. After a bit of moderate weather it is hot and humid again. Hoagie made yoghurt again as usual when I depleted it.  It's threatening to storm this evening. I also picked potato beetle larvae on the hill field and checked bean beetles elsewhere. Yesterday I noted the first bean beetle eggs on pinto beans in the slope garden.

June 20 We had a good shower last night. I froze a qt. of peas and more rain today. I also hoed in the creek garden and we had 16 eggs today.

June 21 I hoed soy beans and Gus hoed corn. I froze a qt. of peas. Only 11 eggs today.

June 22 I hoed soy beans and thinned corn as usual. I hoed lots in beans, tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, squash and pumpkins in the creek garden. I killed potato beetle larvae in both patches. All potatoes look very good, especially in the slope garden. Victor [our beloved cat ] came back after almost 2 weeks absence! 17 eggs today Victor has trouble meowing.

June 23 I cut mostly bush clover hay in the hill field and picked peas. Froze a qt. of peas. I watered tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers in the creek garden and hoed possible corn patch weeds down. 18 eggs today. A few small bean beetle larvae were noted in the creek garden.

June 24 Hauled down a pick-up load of largely bush clover from MM. I hoed potatoes, largely to preserve moisture, as well as soy beans in the hill field. Got involved with a grievance procedure at W.  S. Hospital and got appointed  chairman of the panel.

June 25  I cut and applied mulch to tomatoes in the slope garden. Sold 10 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. to Natural Way store. I watered tomatoes, peppers and cucumbers in the creek garden. Froze 3 pts. of peas. Bought 100 lbs each of corn, oats and mash .

June 26  We had a short shower last night and I watered and hoed in the creek garden. Gus hoed the corn in the slope garden.

June 27  I cut hay on the flood plain and watered in both gardens. i also hoed in the creek garden. The weather was bright, sunny and pleasantly warm, but this evening it's quite cold for this time of year. We have been from the start, feeding our new J.J.Warren chicks ordinary laying mash and they appear to be thriving. Otherwise and other years we had fed them on medicated "grower." We get more than a gallon of milk in the morning and ~ quart from Brenda in the evening. Betty made cheese again-this time a nice little ( to be ) hard cheese in the round.

June 28  Hoagie put paraffin on the cheese and placed it in her cage in the basement. Hope the screen keeps the insects out and nice too. Should be ready to eat in 3-4 weeks. I recommend that we try it on July 28. we hauled in 3 WB full of hay-mostly red clover. i also watered corn, beans and tomatoes and hot peppers in the slope garden.  Gus and I worked at placing plywood  sheeting on the barn[?]  roof. 13 eggs today. We have cool nights and pleasant but dry work days.

June 29  Gus and I  finished placing sheeting on the barn roof. I did a lot of  watering  cucumbers, squash, pumpkins, peppers, tomatoes in the creek garden; beans, tomatoes, peppers and cabbage in the slope garden.  The boys almost finished their second round of corn hoeing and this corn is almost shoulder high now.

June 30 Gus and I papered the barn roof over the SW stall. I watered beets in the creek garden and potatoes and carrots in the slope garden. Ended up placing the hard cheese in the refrigerator; it seem to be oozing a little.

July 1  We had scattered showers today. 14 eggs today. Sold 3 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. Brenda was in heat and Bob and I [ Betty]had to put a board on Leo's fence to keep him from getting to the black lady.

July 2  Rain most of the day. I pulled weeds and thinned sweet corn in the slope garden. The rain should save the crops on the hill. Gus sold some bantams. 19 eggs today.

July 3  Cloudy and rainy off and on all day. 8eggs today.

July 4  Cloudy and rainy during the night again. I pulled weeds in the creek garden in the onion patch and around cabbage and cucumbers. Wilt seems quite bad on the cucumbers, some of which I replanted with Marketer ( Gurney ). 16 eggs today. We had 2 more hard showers this afternoon.

July 5  I dug 2 post holes on the "back" boundary on MM and, with Betty, placed some woven wire on B's boundary. 15 eggs today. Cloudy, humid weather with a few light showers.

July 6 Heavy rain during the night. I went, as chairman, of the panel hearing at WSH on the grievance.

July 7  I dug a posthole and placed some posts on the back boundary. Bought 100lbs each of corn, oats and mash.

Jul 8 Very warm weather. I attended another panel session at WSH, during which the State illegally  halted it when we awarded the decision to the grievants.  15 eggs today. Hoagie made another batch of hard cheese. I noted that, although the heavy rains of the past week seemed to exceed that needed by the garden plants, most seem to have put on much new growth, the beans, for example, put on whole tier of light green leaves 6" or more in height, and the corn grew several feet in some cases. Cabbage, unfortunately, tended to crack somewhat. The apples on PP appear to be about half grown in terms of volume.

Hoagie and I strung some barbed wire on H's boundary and I weeded extensively in the creek garden. 10 eggs today. It's very warm ( ~90s ).

We had a short hard shower this afternoon and a light rain this evening. 10 eggs today. Hoagie went to a bee workshop in Harrisonburg this morning to return Sat. night. ( this being Friday.

I also spread the remainder of the compost on the corn patch there. Planted 9 Stonehead Hybrid cabbage plants in the creek garden. Gus made 10' strip [ part of our land on PP ] impossible to mow on its tip [ by our hostile neighbor ].

May 1  I manure the corn pastch in the slope garden and worked it in after hauling some from Leo's pen. I also planted 9 Stonehead Hybrid cabbage ( K-mart ), 5 in the slope garden over liberal manure and 4 in the creek garden using no fertilizer. I also scraped  the NE wall this morning.. Our feud with our neighbors has heated up again. ie the 10' strip. We observe a high-flying migrating marsh hawk fly right over our yard. Gus planted willow on the 10' strip [ ostensibly to prevent illegal mowing by the hostile neighbor ].

May 2 ( Sun ) I sanded and scraped at the NE house wall and spaded in the garden. Hoagie and I wormed goats again ( All the little ones and Raisin and Jennifer ). Hoagie and I went to the Appalachian  Survival picnic in celebration of their hand clearing the Elliot Knob power line [right-of-way]. The first Whippoorwill sounded tonight. Gus planted willows on [?]

May 3 Planted ~17-100' rows of Reid's Yellow Dent corn on the hill field over goat manure or compost equivalent to ~ 5 tons/acre using planter setting of 32. 8 eggs today.

May 4  I planted a 20' band of Chanteney carrots in the slope garden using no fertilizer. **Note, these were planted in in the same spot I had carrots last year, so they may not do well. I also scraped the NE house wall and spaded in the creek garden. 10 eggs today. It has been freezing lightly each night.

May5 Planted 17-100' rows of Tennessee Red Cob corn in the hill field, using a setting of 35, from my own seed.[was this wise?] Sold a doz. eggs for 80c.  Planted 14 small home-grown Stonehead Hybrid cabbage plants in the slope garden over liberal goat manure. I also spaded in the in the creek garden and scraped the NE house wall. 16 eggs today. The chickens have been eating eggs recently. The weather is pleasant ( ~70s ) but dry.

May 6  Shopping day. Hoagie got Nutmeg back; a student of hers from Craigsville having found her in the company of two other goats. She is here pending notification of her owner. 12 eggs today. It has turned quite warm (~80deg.F ), but I have a terrible cold!

May 7 Sold 2 doz. eggs at 80c a doz.

May 8  I was quite ill but did manage some weeding and other garden work. 23 eggs today, incl. 3 broken. This many resulted because we collected frequently.

May 9 Planted the rest of the field corn = 18-50' rows of Tennessee Red Cob. Alan Harris came and got Nutmeg and Hoagie  presented him with a $30.00 bill. Alan knows next to nothing about goats. Learned that he bought  2 others 9 days after Nutmeg left and all were found together. Candy gave us a big surprise with a filly. She didn't even look pregnant. What a mother's day gift. She was due April 29 and had no bag nor did she look ready. I even had told Richard Wright on May 1 that the horse wasn't going to foal. Don and I carried a quite a bit of water to cabbage, cauliflower, lettuce etc. in the slope garden. I cut the blooms off the rhubarb.

May 10  Sold a doz. eggs for 80c. I prepared ground for sweet corn, clearing weeds and hoed the potatoes, which are doing nicely. in the slope garden. 20 eggs today. December had a buck kid ( Sam ) today.

May 11 over previously spread manure, I planted 6-15' rows of Burpee's  Honeycross and six-15' rows of Early Sunglow sweet corn, using last year's seed, in the slope garden.

May 12  Transplanted tomato plants to pots. 17 eggs today. Temp. is in 80s.. James, a rooster from Vessey's  chicken flock just took Mueller citizenship.

May 13  Planted  two-35' rows of Kidney beans and two 35' rows of Turtle Soup black beans in the creek garden using no fertilizer and a 40' row of Blue Lake bush beans from last year's seed. in the slope garden using no fertilizer. 20 eggs today. I prepared ground ( manured ) in the creek garden. We found out today that Venus had twins.

May 14  Don and I did a lot of watering in the slope garden.    [ start Gus' text ]Gus has a peculiar dark rooster named King Arthur who is a small bantam born in late summer  as one of Pearl's chicks hatched by Ceno. He plans to have culled two other roosters who are tiny and ugly. This trio of roosters, along with Explo actually attack King James, but James can outrun the bantams. King James constantly attempts  to "zap" Gus'  hens, which are about 1/3 his size, but is more successful with Peepedies. King Arthur, the bantam, is the annual replacement for Archo, who was a replacement for Alpha. Explo is an old pet who is always kept, seeing that James "zaps" bantams, has implications of value in Maketna, a larger stock of bantam descendents  of him.[ end Gus' text ]

Potato beetle eggs were on nearly every plant ( which were only a few inches high ) in the slope garden. I disposed of all I saw.

May 15 Planted  twenty-five-100' rows of  Agripro soybeans using a 32 setting on the planter. [ Gus' text follows ]Ginger, a little hen of Gus', had chicks today. At least one chick is a Ceno-type yellow. Under the chickee house is her location. The father could be Archo, Arthur, or the other little roosters. It probably has no relation to expo, who has only zapped Pearl, or a  peepody. ( 4 chicks )

May 16  Planted the rest of the Agripro soy beans  in the hill field using no fertilizer on any of them. Transplanted yesterday and today a 30' row of partly S.H. cabbage and cauliflower in the creek garden over abundant chicken manure. I placed the hoses in the slope garden and watered onions, lettuce, cabbage and cauliflower.

May 17  Planted 9  Cal Wonder sweet pepper plants in the creek garden using no fertilizer. I also continued to scrape the NE house wall. 21 eggs today. Bought 100 lbs each of corn, oats and mash.

May 18  We had a light shower late afternoon yesterday. Planted a 15' band of Chanteney ( FB ) carrots in the slope garden using no fertilizer. Im still enjoying asparagus about every other day. *** There has been a hatch of cicadas ( cycle unknown yet ) and they sing all day as they never have since we've been here. I destroyed many egg masses ( on nearly every plant again ) as well as many adults of potato beetles  in the slope garden. I also prepared places for tomatoes in both gardens and did some watering in the creek garden. 20 eggs today

May 19 Sold 10 doz. eggs for 80c a doz. A nice heavy shower this evening was badly needed even for  germination. 18 eggs today. I did a quite a bit of hoeing of of potatoes, cabbage, onions and peas this afternoon in the slope garden. King James keeps returning to his old kingdom-so he abdicated here![??]

May 20 We disbudded December's buck. Planted 4 VF tomato plants on stakes in the slope garden using no fertilizer. I also weeded and planted more stakes. 23 eggs today.

May 21 Sold 1 doz. eggs for 80c. Planted a 40' row of black Turtle Soup beans in the slope garden on partially fertilized ground. It was  drizzling while I planted. We had a light rain in the afternoon.

May 22  The boys and I hauled over from the PP landing a load of firewood and fence posts.  I planted 5 more VF Hybrid  tomatoes in the slope garden using no fertilizer. Very heavy thundershowers this afternoon.

May 23  The heavy thundershower yesterday did a lot of erosive damage in the hill field, esp. to the soy bean section. I planted  5 VF tomato plants in the creek garden. Pearl is clucking.

May 24  I worked at the NE house wall scraping and did some house cleaning. This evening it has begun to drizzle and lightly rain again after being cloudy the past days. 20 eggs today.

May 25  I weeded in the slope garden and checked fo potato beetles again, killing a fair amount of adults and eggs. The crops in the hill field appear to be doing well, mich more corn sprouting and most beans coming up very vigorously. 21 eggs today.

May 26  Rain during the night. Sold 2 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. I froze 2 qts. of spinach. 24 eggs today, of which 6 were broken and consumed by chickens. It's raining lightly again as I write this. at 9:00 PM.

May 27 Besides going to town shopping, I did some weeding in the gardens. It's raining again this evening as it did last night. 18 eggs today.

May 28  I finally had to hoe in the gardens, although the soil is too wet. I hoed corn and beans in the slope garden and beans in the creek garden. 20 eggs today. It rained again during the night and a little during the day.

May 29  Gus and I fixed the fence by the bees. I hoed the potatoes and beans in the slope garden and potatoes and corn in the hill field. Although there are lots of potato beetles in the slope garden, there are few as yet in the hill field. Also, while  there are abundant flea bettles in the slope garden, there are almost none in the creek garden.

May 30 Fairly heavy rain and thunderstorms much of last evening and night and continuing this morning. Gus, Hoagie and I placed wire along the garden down to the flood plain ready for stretching.  I  weeded and place brush for peas in the slope garden. I picked a qt, of wild strawberries on the PP slope. It's very warm and humid.

May 31  planted 7 hills of Longgreen  and 3 hills of National Pickling cucumbers  over manure in the creek garden and weeded in the hill field. This evening I also hoed ( mostly corn ) in the hill field. 18 eggs today + ~5 eaten by the chickens, who have become very bad.

June 1 Planted 3 hills of butternut ( Waltham ) squash + 2 hills of Small Sugar pumpkin, all over liberal manure in the creek garden. I picked another qt. of wild strawberries and about a pt. of tame. 28 eggs today, incl. two eaten by chickens-I should say at least 28. We castrated December's kid

Jun 2 I cut the hay in the front of the house. Sold 2 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. I  planted 8 Roma VF tomato plants in the slope garden. ~15 eggs today and I hoed lots in the slope garden.

June 3 I cut hay on the stream side of the house and weeded in the hill field. The boys and I hauled 5 WB loads of hay with rain threatening. I did a quite a bit of weeding in the hill field. We had at least 30 eggs today, of which 27 were collected. It's rainiong as I write this-apparently a heavy shower coming.

June 4  Rain off and on, but the goats got up the hill. I fed Leo hay. ~20 eggs today.

June 5  I did a lot of weeding in the hill field soy beans and some in the corn as well as the beans in the creek garden and carrots in the slope garden. Rabbits are very bad on the soy beans. we had at least 24 eggs, but a number were eaten by a bad egg-eating chicken who has survived only because she's an old acquaintance !

June 6 I hoed the corn and cabbage in the slope garden, cleared the rhubarb and horseradish of weeds and hoed 3 rows of soy beans in the hill field. I also scraped the NE side of the house again and sawed and split wood. All vegetation is very lush and the weather very pleasant with cool nights.

June 7  I cut hay in the back yard and hoed soy beans. Planted 17 Cayenne pepper plants. The boys began to hoe corn. Collected 15 eggs, but the chickens ate quite a few others.

June 8  Sold 2 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. Froze a qt. of spinach, hoed soy beans and cut a little grass in the yard. We hauled in one WB of hay. I hoed another row of soy beans  this evening and Don hoed corn. 19 eggs today. The weather has turned hot and humid and I planted 3 VF tomato plants in the slope garden in usual manner.

June 9  Sold 1 doz. eggs for 80c. I hoed 2 rows of soy beans +  1/2  row of corn. I also hoed in the slope garden and picked potato beetles in the slope garden as I did yesterday. 24 eggs today. It's very warm and humid, threatening showers. Froze a qt. of cauliflower.  Light rain this evening halted Gus and I from hoeing on the hill, but Don did some earlier. Some corn is now over knee high and some soy beans are doing well despite the rabbits.

June 10  We had heavy rain last night and scattered showers during the day. 17 eggs today.

June 12  I hoed 2 rows of soy beans, although  the soil was a bit wet. ( Note  it can be hoed on the hill only after a fairly hard rain. ) However it then began to rain and we have been having had showers all day. I froze a pt. of podded peas. Bought 100 lbs each of corn, oats and mash.

June 13 Rain, heavy at times, all night and morning, clearing partially this afternoon. Many mushrooms are appearing. Betty and I picked wild strawberries on PP. 30 eggs today. The stream is surging powerfully and the vegetation is lush.

June 14  Pearl, Hoagie's little black hen, had chicks sometime during  the night. This morning she was seen with a brown-black and a grey-black chick. Wilbur isn't feeling too well in this hot, humid, flea-infested climate of June. The boys and I hoed corn and soy beans and I cut hay in the slope garden and flood plain. I also patched the roof.

I hoed 3 rows of soy beans and Gus and Don each hoed a row of corn. I also hoed sweet corn in the slope garden. 22 eggs today. The weather is turning warmer and more humid again.

June 16  I hoed 1.5 of soy beans and Don and Gus hoed corn.  I also finished hoeing the sweet corn and hoed cucumbers and squash. 23 eggs today. It's very warm and humid.

June 17 [ Here follow details about animals by Gus, not here presented. ]   I thinned corn as usual, although it rained several times today. Hoagie and I went shopping. 23 eggs today and we gave away 8 doz.

June 20 I hoed 2 rows of soy beans and pulled a lot of corn for thinning purposes. Note, I've been storing these thinnings as "hay" in addition to giving them to Leo. The field  corn is now more than knee high on average and some is chest high. The soy beans cover about 1/4 of the ground and some as much as a third, despite heavy rabbit damage.

June 21 ( Summer's first day ) I cut a large patch of hay on the flood plain consisting of a variety of wild plants, but including some red and white clover. The boys hoed corn and I planted about 10 more VF tomato plants in the creek garden. 16 eggs today.

June 22  Hoagie broke a record on milk-~6 qts. Hauled in 4 WB of hay with a few drops falling.  The boys hoed corn and I pulled weeds in the soy beans. The soil is very hard now and difficult to hoe. 19 eggs today. The beans are still in a race with the weeds.

June 23  I cut another patch of hay on the flood plain. Sold 10 doz. eggs at 80c a doz. I began  to mulch tomatoes in the slope garden and the boys hoed corn.

June 24  Gus and I fixed fence, stretching wire along the garden fence.. Don and I  repaired the main flood plain gate and we hauled in 3 WB of hay from the floodplain. 23 eggs today.

June  25  Gus and I fixed fence by the barn and we, incl. Hoagie, rolled a roll of wire up the hill. I weeded the slope garden and watered tomatoes in the same garden. I placed Dipel on the  slope garden cabbage and cauliflower using last year's Dipel. ~16 eggs today.

June 26  I did some hoeing in the creek garden, but mostly we all did house cleaning.  The Bryants visited with a shish ka bob of goat.

June 27  I froze a qt. of peas and hoed and weeded in the creek garden. Sprayed the cabbage and cauliflower with Dipel, thereafter noting extensive  cabbage worm evidence. I also picked bean beetle evidence in the slope garden and weeded the carrots and finished weeding and thinning the sweet corn, the earliest of which is beginning to tassel  now. 17 eggs today.

June 28 Gus and I patched fence on B's boundary  using new woven wire. 18 eggs today. I picked bean beetles again in the slope garden. Noted all cabbage worms in the slope garden dead from the Dipel application. Bought 100 lbs each of corn, oats and mash.

June 28 Light rain this morning and a heavier shower in the afternoon. ~ 16 eggs today.
